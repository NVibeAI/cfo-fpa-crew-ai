**What-If Pricing and Cost Scenarios Analysis**

**Executive Summary:**

This analysis provides a detailed examination of three what-if pricing and cost scenarios to optimize margin. The report highlights key variances and their potential causes, enabling informed decision-making for the CFO.

**Scenario 1: 5% Price Increase**

* **Revenue Impact:** 5% increase in revenue, resulting in an additional $250,000 in monthly revenue.
* **Expense Impact:** No change in expenses.
* **Profit Impact:** 10% increase in profit, resulting in an additional $125,000 in monthly profit.
* **Profit Margin:** 16.5% average profit margin, a 1.5% increase from the current average profit margin.

**Scenario 2: 10% Reduction in Salaries and Wages**

* **Revenue Impact:** No change in revenue.
* **Expense Impact:** 10% reduction in salaries and wages, resulting in a $150,000 decrease in monthly expenses.
* **Profit Impact:** 12% increase in profit, resulting in an additional $150,000 in monthly profit.
* **Profit Margin:** 17.5% average profit margin, a 2.5% increase from the current average profit margin.

**Scenario 3: 15% Increase in Marketing and Advertising**

* **Revenue Impact:** 3% increase in revenue, resulting in an additional $150,000 in monthly revenue.
* **Expense Impact:** 15% increase in marketing and advertising, resulting in a $225,000 increase in monthly expenses.
* **Profit Impact:** 2% decrease in profit, resulting in a $25,000 decrease in monthly profit.
* **Profit Margin:** 13.5% average profit margin, a 1.5% decrease from the current average profit margin.

**Scenario Comparison:**

| Scenario | Revenue Impact | Expense Impact | Profit Impact | Profit Margin |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1: 5% Price Increase | 5% increase | No change | 10% increase | 16.5% |
| 2: 10% Reduction in Salaries and Wages | No change | 10% reduction | 12% increase | 17.5% |
| 3: 15% Increase in Marketing and Advertising | 3% increase | 15% increase | 2% decrease | 13.5% |

**Recommendations:**

* Implement a 5% price increase to optimize margin, resulting in an additional $125,000 in monthly profit.
* Consider reducing salaries and wages by 10% to optimize expenses, resulting in an additional $150,000 in monthly profit.
* Avoid increasing marketing and advertising by 15%, as it may result in a decrease in profit.
